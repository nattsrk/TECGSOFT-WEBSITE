📅 April 2026 📄 v1.0 Estimation 🏢 Central Hospital, India 🔒 Confidential
📋 Executive Summary

Virtual OT is a web-based Digital Operation Theatre platform for Central Hospital. It delivers live multi-camera surgical feeds, AES-256 encrypted recording, AI surgery assistance, voice control, live annotation for remote consultation, role-based access control, and HIPAA/DPDP Act 2023 compliance — all in a single browser-based application requiring no client installation.

⚠ Risks & Assumptions
RiskImpactLikelihoodMitigation
Hospital network latency causing video jitterHighMediumDedicated VLAN for OT traffic, QoS prioritisation, edge buffering
EMR/HIS API unavailability or non-standard HL7MediumHighPhase 4 scope; fallback to manual patient tagging; FHIR adapter layer
Regulatory approval (CDSCO for SaMD classification)HighMediumEngage regulatory consultant from Week 1; design for IEC 62304
Surgical camera compatibility (proprietary SDI/HDMI)MediumMediumHardware capture card (Magewell Pro Capture) as universal adapter
AI Copilot hallucination in clinical contextHighLowRAG over verified medical knowledge base; prominent "not a substitute" disclaimer
Staff adoption resistanceMediumMediumClinical UAT from Week 40, champion surgeon programme, in-app guide
  • UX prototype (v1.1 mock) is approved. No major redesign is expected — saves ~80 PD.
  • Hospital provides dedicated OT network VLAN and IT admin access during setup.
  • Surgical cameras output standard HDMI or SDI. Proprietary capture cards budgeted if needed.
  • Anthropic Claude API is approved for clinical data. Data processed within India-region endpoints.
  • EMR/HIS integration timeline depends on hospital IT team responsiveness (3rd party dependency).
  • CDSCO SaMD regulatory filing is out of scope for Phase 1 but recommended before full hospital rollout.
  • Hardware procurement lead time: allow 4–6 weeks for servers and medical-grade displays.
