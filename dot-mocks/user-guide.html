⚖What is Virtual OT?

Virtual OT (Digital Operation Theatre) is a real-time surgical monitoring and management platform for hospitals. It lets surgical teams, administrators, and remote consultants view live video feeds from multiple operation theatres simultaneously, manage recordings, capture snapshots, generate PDF surgery reports, and use AI-powered tools including voice commands and an intelligent surgery copilot.

Recording
  • 1Click 🔴 Start Recording — the button turns orange and shows ⏹ Stop Recording.
  • 2The recording timer starts counting in the top-right of the video.
  • 3Click ⏹ Stop Recording to stop. The live feed continues playing — stopping recording never interrupts the video.
Important: Stop Recording only stops the recording session — it does NOT stop the live feed. The video keeps running so the team can continue monitoring.
Audio
  • 1All video feeds start muted by default (browser autoplay policy requires this).
  • 2Click 🔇 Unmute to enable audio. Button changes to 🔊 Mute.
  • 3Unmuting applies to all three sources simultaneously (OT, Laparoscope, Endoscope).
Note: When you activate Voice Commands, the video is automatically muted to prevent the mic picking up speaker audio and misinterpreting surgical sounds as commands. Audio is restored when voice is turned off.
Snapshots
  • 1Click 📷 Snapshot in the controls bar (or press S).
  • 2A frame is captured from whichever source is currently visible (OT Camera, Laparoscope, or Endoscope).
  • 3The captured frame appears in the Snapshots panel on the right side of the screen with a timestamp.
  • 4Click ⬇ Save under any snapshot to download it as a .jpg file.
  • 5Click Clear to remove all snapshots from the panel.
Tip: Snapshots are also included in the PDF surgery report generated in the Playback screen.
Play / Pause Video
  • 1Hover over the video to reveal a large circular ▶/⏸ button in the centre.
  • 2Click it to pause or resume. When paused, the button stays visible.
  • 3Alternatively, press Space anywhere on the Live OT page.

Voice Commands AI
  • 1Click 🎙️ Voice in the controls bar (or press V).
  • 2Allow microphone access when the browser asks.
  • 3The button turns red and shows 🔴 Listening… — the video is automatically muted.
  • 4Speak a command clearly. A floating overlay shows what was heard and confirms the action.
  • 5Click again or press V to stop listening.

AI Surgery Copilot AI

The AI Copilot panel appears on the right side of the Live OT screen and tracks the current surgical step in real time.

ElementDescription
Current StepThe detected phase of the surgery (e.g. "Cystic Duct Dissection")
Confidence BarAI confidence score for the current step (green = high)
Event FeedLast 3 confirmed intraoperative events logged by AI

🌐Remote View

Remote View allows consultants, specialists, or senior surgeons to observe the procedure from outside the hospital.

  • 1Select the room to view remotely from the dropdown.
  • 2The live feed streams with a REMOTE watermark to distinguish from the in-room view.
  • 3Use Unmute to hear the OT audio remotely.
  • 4Click Fullscreen for an immersive consultation view.
Enterprise feature: Remote two-way audio and annotation tools are available in the Enterprise tier.

❓FAQ
QuestionAnswer
Why is the video muted by default?Browsers require user interaction before playing audio. Click Unmute after the page loads.
Voice commands aren't workingVoice requires Chrome or Edge browser and HTTPS. It will not work on file:// paths. Check microphone permissions.
Laparoscope/Endoscope shows black screenThe video loads on first click — give it 1–2 seconds to buffer after switching source.
Why does the video mute when I click Voice?To prevent the mic picking up speaker audio (surgical sounds) and misinterpreting them as commands.
Stop Recording stopped my video!It shouldn't — Stop Recording only stops the recording session. The live feed always continues. Try refreshing.
Where is the Snapshot Save button?Take a snapshot first (📷 Snapshot button or S key). A Snapshots panel appears on the right with a ⬇ Save button per frame.
How do I generate a PDF report?Go to Playback → add notes → click "Save Notes & Generate Report". The report panel appears with View and Download options.
